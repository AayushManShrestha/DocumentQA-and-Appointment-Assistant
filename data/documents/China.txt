China
Capital: Beijing
Population: Over 1.4 billion
Official Language: Mandarin Chinese
Currency: Renminbi (Yuan - CNY)

Geography:
China is the fourth-largest country in the world by area and features an array of natural landscapes—mountains, rivers, deserts, and coastlines. It shares borders with 14 countries including Russia, India, Pakistan, and Vietnam. The country is divided into 23 provinces, 5 autonomous regions, and 4 direct-controlled municipalities.

History:
China’s civilization dates back over 5,000 years. The Xia, Shang, and Zhou dynasties were early examples of complex state formation. The Qin dynasty (from which the country gets its name) unified China and began the construction of the Great Wall. Later dynasties like Han, Tang, Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing shaped Chinese culture and politics. The People's Republic of China was established in 1949 after a civil war.

Culture:
China's cultural heritage includes Confucianism, Taoism, calligraphy, traditional medicine, and culinary diversity. It is famous for inventions such as paper, gunpowder, the compass, and printing. Major festivals include Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival, and Dragon Boat Festival.

Economy:
China is the world’s second-largest economy by nominal GDP and the largest by purchasing power parity. It is a manufacturing and export powerhouse with key industries in electronics, textiles, machinery, and pharmaceuticals. China's Belt and Road Initiative is a global infrastructure project aimed at improving trade and investment across continents.
